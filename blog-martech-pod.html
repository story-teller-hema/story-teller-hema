Building a Martech Pod: Scaling Adobe Analytics at Enterprise Level

October 2025‚Ä¢10 min read
Managing 2 FTE and 5 embedded consultants across three continents taught me everything about pod structure, agile ceremonies, vendor partnerships, and talent development. Here's the operating model that powers analytics at scale.

The Challenge: Enterprise Analytics Without Enterprise Chaos

When you're running Adobe Analytics, Customer Journey Analytics, and Target for a global QSR brand, the tech stack is the easy part. The hard part is the people, processes, and politics. How do you deliver 50+ concurrent experiments without breaking prod? How do you align stakeholders across Digital, Marketing, CRM, Product, and Tech? How do you scale without becoming a bottleneck?

The answer: a well-structured pod operating model.

```

Pod Structure: 2 FTE + 5 Consultants

FTE Strategists (2): Own roadmap, stakeholder management, business requirements, and outcomes. We're the "what" and "why."

Embedded Consultants (5): Execute tagging, experimentation, analytics builds, and QA. They're the "how." Embedded means they sit in our sprints, report to our backlog, and have SLAs we own.

Why this split? Strategy can't scale if strategists are writing SDRs and debugging Launch rules. Execution can't succeed without clear requirements and business context. The pod bridges both.

Agile Ceremonies That Actually Work

Weekly Sprint Planning (Mondays, 60 min): Review backlog, prioritize top 10 items, assign to consultants, flag blockers. No status updates‚Äîthose happen async in Azure DevOps.

Daily Standups (15 min): Only for active projects. Answer three questions: What shipped yesterday? What's shipping today? What's blocked? If nothing's blocked, skip it.

Bi-Weekly Backlog Grooming (Thursdays, 45 min): Refine upcoming work, estimate effort, kill zombie tickets that will never ship. This is where we say "no" to low-ROI requests.

Monthly Business Reviews (Last Friday, 90 min): Present results to senior leadership. Show metrics that matter: conversion lifts, revenue impact, test velocity. No vanity metrics.

üí° The "No Meeting" Rule

We protect 3-hour blocks (Tue/Wed/Thu mornings) where no meetings are scheduled. Deep work requires uninterrupted time. Strategists use this for roadmap planning. Consultants use it for complex implementations. Productivity doubled when we enforced this.

Vendor Management: Partners, Not Vendors

Our consultants came from a Martech agency. The key to success: treating them as partners, not outsourced labor.

  • Co-own outcomes: They have skin in the game. If conversion doesn't lift, we troubleshoot together.
  • Invest in training: Paid for Adobe certifications, sent them to Summit, included them in strategy discussions.
  • Clear SLAs: 48hr turnaround for tagging tickets, 1-week for experiment builds, 24hr for production bugs. No ambiguity.
  • Feedback loops: Quarterly retros to discuss what's working, what's not, and how to improve collaboration.

Talent Development: Growing the Team

How do you retain top talent in Martech when everyone's recruiting?

1. Career pathing: Junior consultants ‚Üí Senior consultants ‚Üí Strategic advisors. Clear progression.

2. Skill diversification: Rotate people across Analytics, Target, CJA, and AJO so they don't get pigeonholed.

3. Visibility to leadership: Bring consultants to business reviews. Let them present their work. Recognition matters.

4. Autonomy: Senior consultants own entire workstreams (e.g., "You own all kiosk analytics"). Ownership drives engagement.

The Backlog: How We Prioritize

With 100+ stakeholder requests at any time, prioritization is everything. Our framework:

Tier 1 (Do Now): Revenue-blocking bugs, exec commitments, regulatory requirements.

Tier 2 (Do This Quarter): High-impact tests, strategic initiatives aligned to OKRs.

Tier 3 (Do Someday/Maybe): Nice-to-haves, exploratory work, "someone mentioned this once" ideas.

We kill Tier 3 ruthlessly. If it hasn't moved to Tier 2 in 3 months, it's not important.

Governance: Standards Without Bureaucracy

Enterprise analytics needs guardrails, not red tape.

  • SDRs (Solution Design References): Every implementation has one. Non-negotiable.
  • Analytics Playbooks: Region-specific guides for tagging standards, naming conventions, QA checklists.
  • Consent Frameworks: GDPR/CCPA compliance baked into every implementation from day one.
  • Code Reviews: No Launch rule or Target activity ships without peer review. Catches 90% of bugs pre-prod.

Cross-Functional Alignment

Martech pods don't exist in isolation. We partnered closely with:

Product: Aligned on roadmap, ensured analytics instrumentation was part of feature specs.

Engineering: Embedded in their sprints for tagging releases, participated in architecture reviews.

CRM/Loyalty: Co-owned journey orchestration, shared audience segmentation logic.

Media: Provided attribution data, collaborated on UTM standards and campaign tracking.

üß† The Weekly Sync That Changed Everything

We instituted a weekly 30-min sync with Product, Engineering, and CRM leads. Just alignment‚Äîno decisions, no deep dives. Reduced miscommunication by 80%. Most "urgent" requests were solved in that room before becoming tickets.

Lessons Learned

1. Hire for adaptability: Martech changes constantly. Hire people who learn fast, not people who know one tool perfectly.

2. Document relentlessly: Tribal knowledge kills scale. Every process, every decision, every edge case gets documented.

3. Celebrate wins publicly: When a test drives $500K revenue, tell everyone. Martech is often invisible work‚Äîmake it visible.

4. Say no often: Your job isn't to do everything. It's to do the right things. Protect the team's capacity.

5. Automate the boring stuff: Reporting, dashboards, recurring QA checks‚Äîautomate it. Free up brainpower for strategy.

The Results

In 18 months, this pod structure enabled:

  • 50+ concurrent A/B tests without production incidents
  • Deployment velocity: 2-week average from request to launch
  • Zero consultant turnover (industry average is 40% annually)
  • Stakeholder satisfaction score: 4.7/5
  • Revenue impact: $10M+ attributed to optimization initiatives

Operating models aren't sexy. But they're the difference between a Martech team that's constantly firefighting and one that's driving business outcomes. Build the structure right, and the results follow.
```
